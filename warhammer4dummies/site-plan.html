Overview

Purpose

When I say Warhammer, do you immediately think about how complicated and time-consuming it is? Do you wish there was a more concise and user-friendly way to look into the hobby? That is what my website is designed to do. After almost 11 years in the hobby myself, I have developed this website to explain the hobby and give advice to new players and hobbyists in an effort to ease them comfortably into a rather daunting universe.

Audience

The intended audience are those who are interested in joining a world-wide community of hobby enthusiasts. There is a scene for anyone such as competitive tournaments, open play, campaign-style games, and everything in between. This site is meant to help those wanting to start into the hobby by offering some insight into what is needed to start building and playing the game as well as some instructions on how the game is played in layman’s terms. This is not meant to be a completely comprehensive and in-depth instruction guide, there is an official website for that. This will be just to get someone interested in the hobby basic information to help them decide if it is for them.

Content

Home page

This website will contain pertinent information for the table-top wargame, Warhammer 40000. Those who stumble upon it will be those who are looking for the easiest way to get into the hobby without having to jump in headfirst. Warhammer 40000, and all the various iterations of the game, is published by Games Workshop. Based out of the UK, they have become a world-wide phenomenon with shops located in many cities across the world. The first thing you should understand is there have been 9 editions since 1987. Back then, the game was called “Rogue Trader” and the rules were closer to a Role-Playing Game (RPG) than the table-top wargame it transformed into later. There were less constraints about weapon choices, vehicles loadouts, or armies available to players. Perhaps one of the biggest changes would be the use of a Game Master (GM). New editions do not require any such figure, though it is not against any written rules. Many of the armies available to players now were not featured until later editions, instead they had other armies that faded out as the years progressed. Armies like the “Squats”, dwarf-like humanoids known for being tough and wild, or “Slann”, amphibius humanoids that featured more in the fantasy editions when Game Workshop decided to split their “40k” universe from the more fantasy universe.

How to Build and Paint

When deciding which army is best for you, a good rule of thumb is to go with the army you think looks cool. This way you can begin to play with an army that you enjoy. You may decide that army is not what you are looking for, and that is fine! You learned how to play, that is the most important part. Each army has kits that may be purchased. These can be expensive, so keep your eye out for deals and “hand-me-downs” on Facebook or KSL. Second-hand is usually the cheapest way to begin collecting your chosen army, however the models are usually already put together with weapons the seller decided on. My suggestion here would be to try to find a small army being sold by a private party, then supplement it with new boxes from your local game store. Next to think about is your color scheme. This can be tricky. Pinterest is a good resource for ideas. I like to think about the army. For Tyranids, the “bugs”, I would look at nature. What have our insects done to show they are dangerous, or to scare away predators? There is inspiration everywhere if you know where to look. A good set of paints are from Citadel, they are the Games Workshop specific paint line, which does mean they can be pricey. Vallejo is another good paint line, not as pricey, but not as many colors to choose from. Unfortunately any basic craft store paint can be too thick and require some modifications in order to use.

How to Play

There are 3 main modes of play. Narrative: This is an organized campaign that allows players to build their army as the army grows within the campaign. This is a fun way to get to know players of every level, from the most experienced to the newest of players. Open War: This mode involves the use of special cards that offer a twist in the original gameplay. Three cards are drawn initially, one from the Deployment deck, one from the Objectives deck, and one from the Twists deck. There are two other decks that can be used if specific circumstances are met, those are the Ruse and the Sudden Death decks. These last two are only used if your opponent has a larger army than your own. This mode is a very fun way to play different missions than what is in the core rule book or any of the other supplements that have been published. Matched Play: This mode encompasses tournaments and any other organized method of play. This mode is for more serious players, those that want a more structured game play with specific missions and rules. This can be fun, especially if the opponent is good natured.
